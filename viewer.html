Project Overview

A computational pipeline that predicts novel drug resistance mutations in Mycobacterium tuberculosis using multi-stage machine learning, then structurally validates the top candidates with AutoDock Vina molecular docking.

Dataset: CRyPTIC consortium — 1,037 Mtb genomes, 13 drugs, 305 tiered mutations

Next Steps & Limitations

Immediate Actions

  • Submit inhA I16V + rpsL K43E to CRyPTIC consortium for experimental MIC testing
  • Run MD free energy perturbation (OpenMM/GROMACS) for top 3 candidates
  • Expand docking to include flexible receptor (Vina 1.2 flexible sidechains)
  • Cross-reference with CARD / ReSeqTB for any missed literature

Pipeline Limitations

  • Vina rigid-receptor underestimates ddG for small/large mutations
  • NADH/NAD+, STR, AMK exceed Vina tree.h torsion limit (complex branched rings)
  • Indirect mechanisms (gyrB Q538L, allosteric rpoB) invisible to static docking
  • AlphaFold models lack co-crystal conformational context
  • Loss-of-function (pncA, katG) requires enzyme activity assay, not docking

Longer Term

  • Whole-genome MD simulations with DNA for gyrB/gyrA water-bridge analysis
  • Include rrs (16S rRNA) mutations for aminoglycoside resistance prediction
  • Train on larger WHO catalogue v2.0 when released
  • Incorporate AlphaFold2 pLDDT as confidence weight per mutation

Manuscript Opportunities

  • inhA I16V — undocumented Val substitution at I16 position (I16T known, not V)
  • rpsL K43E — charge reversal at #1 STR resistance residue (K43R known, not E)
  • eis V59A — coding region resistance in promoter-regulated gene (all known = promoter)
  • rpoB V170I — outside RRDR, low-level RIF via allosteric dynamics (V170F known)
  • gyrB Q538L — Mtb-specific TOPRIM water-bridge disruption (N538D in E. coli only)
